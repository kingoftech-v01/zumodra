Dispute Resolution Process

1. Terms

FreelanHub is committed to helping freelancers and clients resolve disputes fairly and efficiently. This Dispute Resolution Process outlines the steps to address conflicts that may arise during transactions, project work, or platform interactions.

Common disputes on our platform include payment disagreements over milestone completion, quality concerns regarding delivered work, scope disputes when project requirements are unclear, deadline conflicts, communication breakdowns, and violations of our Terms of Service. Our process is designed to protect both parties while facilitating constructive resolution.

We encourage users to attempt direct communication first before escalating disputes. Many issues can be resolved through professional dialogue. However, when direct resolution fails, our structured dispute process provides a clear pathway to fair outcomes with support from our mediation team.

2. Limitations

When a dispute arises, users should initiate the formal dispute process through their project dashboard. The process follows these structured steps:

  • Informal Resolution (1-3 days): Both parties attempt to resolve the issue through direct communication using our messaging system. During this period, funds in escrow remain protected and no actions are taken against either party's account.

  • Mediation (5-7 days): If informal resolution fails, either party can request mediation. A neutral FreelanHub mediator reviews all evidence, communicates with both parties, and proposes a fair resolution based on our Terms of Service and industry standards.

  • Arbitration (10-14 days): For unresolved disputes, binding arbitration is available. An independent arbitrator reviews the complete case record and issues a final, enforceable decision. This decision is binding on both parties and cannot be appealed.

Throughout the process, escrow funds remain protected until resolution. We encourage good faith participation at all stages to reach fair outcomes quickly. Delays or non-participation may negatively impact the final decision.

3. Revisions and errata

Strong evidence is crucial for successful dispute resolution. When filing a dispute, users should gather and submit comprehensive documentation to support their position. The quality and completeness of evidence significantly impacts the outcome of mediation and arbitration.

Required evidence includes all written communications between parties through our messaging system, the original project description and agreed scope, contracts or service agreements, deliverables submitted by the freelancer, revision requests and feedback, payment records and milestone details, screenshots of relevant conversations or work, and any other documentation demonstrating the facts of the dispute.

Evidence must be submitted through our secure dispute portal within the specified timeframes. Late submissions may not be considered. Both parties have equal opportunity to present evidence and respond to claims. Our mediators and arbitrators evaluate evidence objectively based on contractual obligations and platform policies.

4. Site terms of use modifications

Dispute outcomes vary based on the specific circumstances and evidence presented. Our mediation and arbitration teams have authority to implement various resolutions to achieve fair outcomes:

  • Payment adjustments including full payment to freelancer, partial payment based on work completed, refund to client, or split decisions where both parties share costs based on fault determination.

  • Account actions such as warnings, temporary restrictions, rating adjustments, or in severe cases of misconduct, permanent account suspension for repeated violations or fraudulent behavior.

  • Additional remedies including mandated project completion, revision requirements, deadline extensions, or requirements for future compliance with specific terms to prevent recurring issues.

All decisions are documented and communicated to both parties with detailed reasoning. Arbitration decisions are final and binding. Users who fail to comply with arbitration outcomes may face account suspension and legal action to enforce the decision.

5. Risks

FreelanHub's dispute resolution process is designed to handle the vast majority of conflicts efficiently and fairly. However, in rare cases involving complex legal issues, significant financial amounts, or disputes that cannot be resolved through our process, parties may have the right to pursue legal action.

Before pursuing litigation, users must complete our dispute resolution process in good faith. This requirement is outlined in our Terms of Service. Legal action should be considered a last resort after all platform-based resolution attempts have been exhausted. Users are responsible for their own legal costs and must comply with applicable jurisdiction requirements.

We strongly encourage users to prevent disputes by maintaining clear communication, documenting agreements thoroughly, setting realistic expectations, using our milestone-based escrow system, providing detailed project specifications, and adhering to professional standards throughout the working relationship. Prevention is always better than resolution.
