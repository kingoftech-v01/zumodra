Service Agreement

1. Terms

This Service Agreement governs the relationship between freelancers and clients conducting business through FreelanHub. When a client accepts a freelancer's proposal or when parties agree to work together on the platform, a binding service contract is formed. This agreement defines the terms, expectations, and obligations for both parties throughout the engagement.

Services are typically structured around milestones with defined deliverables, timelines, and payment terms. Each milestone should clearly specify what work will be completed, acceptance criteria, and payment amount. Both parties are encouraged to communicate openly and document all agreements and changes through the platform's messaging system to maintain a clear record.

The service contract exists between the freelancer and client directly. FreelanHub facilitates the connection and provides tools for project management, communication, and secure payment processing, but is not a party to the service agreement and bears no responsibility for work quality, delivery, or outcomes.

2. Limitations

FreelanHub's role is strictly limited to providing a marketplace platform and related services. The following limitations apply to the platform's involvement in service agreements:

  • FreelanHub is an intermediary and technology provider only. We do not create an employment relationship, agency, or partnership between freelancers, clients, or the platform.

  • Freelancers are independent contractors responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and legal obligations. Clients are responsible for determining project requirements and evaluating freelancer qualifications.

  • The platform does not guarantee work quality, freelancer availability, timely delivery, or client satisfaction. Users must exercise their own due diligence when selecting work partners.

All intellectual property created during the engagement belongs to the parties as agreed in their service contract. Platform fees apply as outlined in our Payment Terms and are separate from the compensation agreed between freelancer and client.

3. Revisions and errata

Changes to the scope, timeline, or terms of a service agreement must be mutually agreed upon by both the freelancer and client. All modifications should be documented through the platform's messaging system or by creating revised milestone agreements to maintain a clear record and prevent misunderstandings.

If disputes arise regarding deliverables, quality, or other aspects of the service agreement, parties are encouraged to communicate directly to resolve issues. If direct resolution fails, either party may initiate FreelanHub's dispute resolution process as outlined in our Dispute Resolution policy. The platform will review evidence and facilitate mediation.

FreelanHub reserves the right to update this Service Agreement framework to comply with legal requirements or improve platform functionality. Users will be notified of material changes, and ongoing projects will generally be governed by the terms in effect when the project began unless both parties agree otherwise.

4. Site terms of use modifications

Either party may terminate a service agreement according to the terms specified in their contract. FreelanHub provides tools to facilitate orderly project closure. The following termination scenarios are recognized:

  • Mutual agreement: Both parties agree to end the engagement. Payments for completed milestones are processed, and incomplete work is handled as agreed between parties.

  • Unilateral termination: One party ends the contract according to cancellation terms. Early termination fees or partial payments may apply based on work completed.

  • Platform-initiated termination: FreelanHub may terminate agreements that violate platform policies, with funds distributed based on completed work and dispute resolution findings.

Upon termination, parties should deliver completed work, process final payments, and provide necessary project documentation. Both parties retain rights to work completed before termination unless otherwise specified in their agreement. Platform fees for completed transactions remain non-refundable.

5. Risks

While FreelanHub provides an escrow system to secure payments, users should understand that freelance engagements carry inherent risks. Work quality may vary, deadlines may not be met, and disagreements over deliverables can occur. Both parties should clearly define expectations, maintain detailed communication, and document all agreements to minimize these risks.

FreelanHub's liability is strictly limited to platform service provision. We are not liable for work quality, professional negligence, intellectual property disputes, or financial losses resulting from service agreements between users. The platform provides dispute resolution services but cannot guarantee outcomes that satisfy all parties.

We strongly recommend that users maintain appropriate insurance coverage for their professional activities. Freelancers should consider professional liability insurance, and clients should evaluate whether contractor agreements require additional protections. For high-value or complex projects, parties may wish to supplement platform agreements with formal contracts reviewed by legal counsel.
